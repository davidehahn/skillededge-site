01 Pre-Treatment Patient Assessment Checklist
Medical History Review
  • Allergies confirmed (especially albumin/eggs)
  • No neuromuscular disorders (ALS, myasthenia gravis)
  • No current infections at injection sites
  • Medication review completed
  • Blood thinners / anticoagulants noted
  • Muscle relaxants noted
  • Aminoglycoside antibiotics noted
  • Not pregnant or breastfeeding
  • Previous Botox treatments documented
  • Date of last treatment: ___________
  • Units used previously: ___________
Consultation & Consent
  • Patient goals and concerns discussed
  • Realistic outcomes explained
  • Common side effects reviewed
  • Bruising and swelling (3–7 days)
  • Onset timeline (3–7 days, full effect 14 days)
  • Duration (3–6 months typical)
  • Rare risks discussed (ptosis, asymmetry)
  • Consent form signed and filed
  • Before photos taken (3 angles minimum)
  • Frontal: neutral expression
  • Frontal: full expression (raise brows)
  • Lateral: 45 degree angle

Preparation Checklist
  • Verify product: name, lot number, expiration
  • Refrigerated storage confirmed (2–8°C)
  • Preservative-free 0.9% saline only
  • Reconstitute gently — do not shake
  • Draw up using correct syringe size
  • Label syringe with date, time, units/mL
  • Use within 24 hours of reconstitution
  • Record lot number in patient chart
Supplies Needed
30–32G needle
1 mL syringe
Alcohol swabs
Gauze
Ice pack
Topical numbing

04 Treatment Procedure Checklist
Room Setup & Patient Prep
  • Treatment bed at 45-degree angle
  • Good lighting confirmed (overhead + side)
  • All supplies laid out and within reach
  • Patient hair pulled back / secured
  • Makeup removed from treatment areas
  • Topical numbing applied if requested
  • Wait time: _______ minutes
  • Skin cleaned with alcohol swab
  • Alcohol dried completely before injection
  • Treatment zones marked if using mapping
Injection Execution
  • Start with least sensitive zone first
  • Inject intramuscularly at correct depth
  • Aspirate if using our protocol: ☐ Yes ☐ No
  • Apply gentle pressure after each injection
  • Do not massage treatment areas
  • Assess symmetry between zones
  • Patient comfort checked throughout
  • After photos taken (matching before angles)
  • Total units used documented in chart
  • Lot number recorded in patient record
05 Patient Aftercare Instructions
First 4 Hours
  • Stay upright — no lying down
  • No touching or rubbing treated areas
  • No strenuous exercise
  • No alcohol
  • No facial massages
First 24 Hours
  • Avoid saunas and hot tubs
  • No intense cardio exercise
  • Sleep on back if possible
  • Avoid blood thinners (aspirin, ibuprofen)
  • Gentle cleanser only on face
Ongoing Instructions
  • Results visible in 3–7 days
  • Full effect at 14 days
  • 2-week follow-up offered
  • Next appointment: 3–4 months
  • Contact us if any concerns
06 Common Mistakes to Avoid
  • Injecting too high on forehead — risks brow drop and heavy appearance
  • Over-treating the forehead alone — always balance with glabella
  • Too many units in one zone — frozen appearance, patient dissatisfaction
  • Massaging after injection — spreads toxin to unintended muscles
  • Ignoring asymmetry baseline — always document pre-existing asymmetry
  • Not checking brow position — assess before treating to avoid ptosis
  • Skipping the 2-week follow-up — this is where you assess and touch up
  • Under-documenting — exact units per zone must be in the chart
