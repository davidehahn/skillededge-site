Where your shop is leaking

Busy machines, thin margins. Here's why.

CNC shops have tools and ERPs — but operators don't consistently feed them good data or execute correctly. Here's what that costs you every year.

Quoting accuracy gaps

One bad estimate on a complex multi-axis job can eat an entire month's margin. Without quote-to-actual tracking, you don't know which job families are bleeding you.

Setup variability

Your best machinist sets up in 45 minutes. Your newest operator takes 2.5 hours on the same job. That gap is documented process — and it's completely transferable.

Veteran knowledge walking out the door

When your lead machinist retires, 30 years of Fanuc and Mazak institutional knowledge goes with them. That's a $50K–$100K event in lost throughput and ramp time.

First article and callback costs

Every part that fails first article or comes back for rework is paid labor with zero output. A 5-point pre-run checklist eliminates most of these.

Slow operator ramp

New machinists at 60% productivity for 6+ months is a recurring drag. Documented setup playbooks cut that to 6–8 weeks in most shops.

No enforcement visibility

You can't hold operators accountable for setup time or first article pass rate without tracking it. The system only works if you can see who's following it.

🧠

The veteran departure problem is bigger than you think.

Ask your lead machinist why they adjust a parameter at a certain point and you'll hear "I just know" or "it feels right." They're not being evasive — decades of pattern recognition compressed into instinct. Extracting that knowledge requires observation, not interviews. CNCVault's D-Series capture methodology documents it before it walks out the door and turns it into onboarding material your next hire uses day one.
